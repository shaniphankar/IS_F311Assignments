drawTree function:

It divides the length into two parts and branches it at different angles (30,60,etc) and does it recursively.


Remarks:

  • The timings are mostly as expected. The Crystal and Pentaplexity figures have high recursion depth and end up taking a lot of time. This can be observed in the Composite curves as well.
  • Our algorithms calculate some intermediate floating point values. We found it was not always possible to find a clever way to force our drawing to be int only. However, since we reused most of our lines and circle code(which were optimized using integers only) when drawing these fractals, our code runs pretty fast.

Issues:

  • Initially, attempts were made to make Point, Line and Circle objects with their own drawing functions defined within. However, the nature of OpenGL display function caused problems with this implementation. Later, a more straightforward implementation was chosen.
  • The Line Drawing algorithm was initially very troublesome with negatively sloped lines. Later, corrections were made to account for this shortcoming. This includes: accounting for negative values of dx and dy, changing increment sign depending on relative location of points etc.
  • The recursive circle fractals have a few trailing points along y-axis. Current Hypothesis is that they are centers of circles that aren’t being plotted for some reason.
